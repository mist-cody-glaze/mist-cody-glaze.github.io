Trip Planning Guide

Riding the San Francisco Municipal Railway (Muni) is a convenient and efficient way to get around the city. Whether you're a resident or a visitor, here are step-by-step instructions on how to ride Muni:

Step 1: Plan Your Trip

Before heading out, determine your starting point, destination, and the Muni lines that will take you there. You can use various tools, including the official SFMTA (San Francisco Municipal Transportation Agency) website, Google Maps, or a dedicated public transit app to plan your route.



SFMTA Trip Planner Google Maps

Step 2: Payment Options

Muni accepts various payment methods, and the most common is the Clipper Card, a contactless smart card used for multiple Bay Area transit agencies. You can also use mobile payment apps or purchase single-ride tickets, monthly passes, or cable car tickets, depending on your needs.


Step 3: Boarding The Vehicle

Boarding locations depend on the type of transit you are taking:

  • At Muni Metro (light rail) stations, head to the platform and wait for the next train. Make sure you're on the right platform for your direction of travel.

  • At bus stops, check the posted schedule for the next arrival time, and stand near the sign that corresponds to your bus line.

  • When riding the iconic cable cars, queue up at the designated turnaround or stop. Cable cars often have a conductor who will collect your fare.


Step 4: Entering the Vehicle

Entering depends on the vehicle:

  • On Muni buses and light rail vehicles, enter through the front door and pay your fare or tap your Clipper Card. If you're using a paper transfer, request one from the driver upon boarding.

  • On cable cars, you'll pay the conductor onboard. Exact change is recommended.


Step 5: Seating and Etiquette
  1. Find a seat or standing spot, but be considerate of other passengers, particularly those who may need priority seating.

  2. Yield seats to seniors, disabled individuals, and pregnant passengers, as required by law.

  3. If you have mobility challenges, look for low-floor buses or light rail vehicles, which are equipped with ramps or lifts.

  4. Stay informed by using real-time arrival apps or monitoring digital signs at transit stations to track your ride's progress.

  5. Sit back and enjoy your journey. San Francisco's diverse neighborhoods and scenic views make for an interesting and picturesque commute.

warning While San Francisco is generally a safe city, be aware of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings. Report any suspicious activity to the operator or a station agent. Report any suspicious activity to the operator or a station agent.


Step 6: Exiting
  • The last and final step is to exit the vehicle once you've reached your destination
  • To notify the driver that you would like to exit, simply pull on the yellow wire near the vehicle's windows or press one of the "STOP" buttons located on the vehicle hand poles.
